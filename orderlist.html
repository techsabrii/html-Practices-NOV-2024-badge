White Karahi, also known as White Chicken Karahi, is a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced dish from the heart of Pakistani cuisine. Unlike traditional red karahi, this version is made without tomatoes or red chilies, giving it a distinct ivory hue and a delicate, buttery flavor. Cooked in a traditional wok-style pan called a “karahi,” this dish features tender bone-in chicken simmered in yogurt, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and a blend of mild aromatic spices. The dish is then enriched with fresh cream and butter or ghee, which creates its signature creamy texture and luxurious taste. White Karahi is perfect for those who enjoy rich, flavorful curries without the overpowering heat. It pairs beautifully with naan, roti, or jeera rice, and is often garnished with julienned ginger, chopped coriander, and fresh green chilies for a fresh finish.
